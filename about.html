Where it all began

With the ACT fast approaching, I knew that I needed to find a productive place to study. My first instinct was to head to my local public library. Upon entering, I immediately noticed homeless individuals, the stench of urine and most importantly the lack of students that are my age. At that moment I knew I couldn't study there. My second instinct was to head towards Columbia University's Butler Hall to see if I could study at one of their facilities. I walked up to the information desk and asked if they had any opportunities for NYC natives to study at Columbia. I was met with an abrupt, "no such program exists." That's where we come in...

We grant high school scholars access to university libraries during the summer. Students can apply for access through our website. Scholars will have to provide: proof of residence and a letter of recommendation from a teacher.

Administrator's have the ability to confirm or deny the applicant through their own exclusive page. If approved, Summer Study Space will send the student a $30 entrance fee. In exchange, the student will receive a digital pass that permits access to that space. If denied, the applicant will receive a rejection letter.
